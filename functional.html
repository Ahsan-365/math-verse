Norms & Metric Spaces

A norm ‖·‖ on a vector space satisfies: ‖v‖≥0 (=0 iff v=0), ‖αv‖=|α|‖v‖, ‖u+v‖≤‖u‖+‖v‖.

Lᵖ norms (on ℝⁿ):
‖v‖₁ = Σ|vᵢ| (taxicab/Manhattan) | ‖v‖₂ = (Σvᵢ²)^{1/2} (Euclidean) | ‖v‖ₚ = (Σ|vᵢ|ᵖ)^{1/p}
‖v‖∞ = max|vᵢ| (supremum norm) | ‖v‖ₚ↓‖v‖∞ as p→∞
Hölder's inequality: |⟨u,v⟩| ≤ ‖u‖_p · ‖v‖_q (1/p+1/q=1)
Completeness: Banach space = complete normed space. L^p(μ) is Banach for p∈[1,∞].

Hilbert Spaces — Inner Product & Orthogonality

A Hilbert space H is a complete inner product space. The inner product ⟨·,·⟩ induces a norm ‖v‖=√⟨v,v⟩.

Key properties:
Cauchy-Schwarz: |⟨u,v⟩|² ≤ ⟨u,u⟩⟨v,v⟩
Parallelogram law: ‖u+v‖²+‖u-v‖²=2(‖u‖²+‖v‖²) (characterizes inner product spaces)
Projection: proj_v(u) = ⟨u,v⟩/‖v‖² · v
Riesz representation: Every bounded linear functional = inner product with some fixed vector
Orthonormal basis: ⟨eᵢ,eⱼ⟩=δᵢⱼ. Parseval: ‖f‖²=Σ|⟨f,eₙ⟩|²

Bounded Linear Operators

T: H₁→H₂ is bounded if ‖T‖ = sup{‖Tv‖ : ‖v‖≤1} < ∞. Equivalently: T is continuous.

Operator norm calculator:
For matrix A ∈ ℝⁿˣⁿ: ‖A‖₂ = σ_max(A) (largest singular value)
‖A‖₁ = max column sum | ‖A‖∞ = max row sum | ‖A‖_F = (Σaᵢⱼ²)^{1/2} (Frobenius)
