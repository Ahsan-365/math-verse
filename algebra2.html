Abstract Algebra II — Rings & Fields

Ring ℤₙ — Arithmetic & Properties

A ring (R, +, ·) has two operations. If every non-zero element has a multiplicative inverse, it's a field.

Field Extensions & Finite Fields GF(pⁿ)

Key facts:
A finite field GF(q) exists iff q = pⁿ for prime p.
GF(p) = ℤ_p (integers mod p). |GF(pⁿ)| = pⁿ.
GF(pⁿ) is the splitting field of x^(pⁿ)−x over GF(p).
All finite fields of the same order are isomorphic.

Polynomial Rings ℤ[x] and GF(p)[x]

Polynomials over a ring form a ring. Over a field, GCD and irreducibility are well-defined via Euclidean algorithm.

Ideals of ℤₙ & Quotient Rings

Key theorems:
Every ideal of ℤ is principal: I = (n) = nℤ.
Quotient ring ℤ/nℤ ≅ ℤₙ.
(n) is maximal iff n is prime (ℤₙ is then a field).
(n) is prime iff n is 0 or prime.
Chinese Remainder Theorem: ℤ/mnℤ ≅ ℤ/mℤ × ℤ/nℤ if gcd(m,n)=1.
