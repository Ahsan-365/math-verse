Direct Proof — "If P then Q"

Assume P is true, then through a logical sequence of steps, show Q must also be true.

Proof by Contrapositive — Prove (¬Q ⟹ ¬P)

"P ⟹ Q" is logically equivalent to "¬Q ⟹ ¬P". Sometimes proving the contrapositive is easier.

Mathematical Induction

Prove P(1) (base case), then prove P(k) ⟹ P(k+1) (inductive step). Concluded: P(n) holds for all n ≥ 1.

Proof by Contradiction — "Reductio ad Absurdum"

Assume ¬P is true, then derive a contradiction (something known to be false). Therefore P must be true.
